The Challenge Before Us

Systemic Issues with Current AI Models

  • Factual Unreliability: Large language models frequently generate inaccurate information, drawing from unvetted sources across the internet
  • Information Currency: AI responses often rely on outdated data, providing users with obsolete information

The Existential Threat to Public Media

  • Audience Migration: AI chatbots are increasingly substituting for direct website engagement, undermining both membership and underwriting models
  • The Substitution Effect: Research suggests approximately 80% of information queries can now be satisfied through conversational AI
  • Disintermediation Risk: Even when AI systems provide source attribution, users rarely engage with the original content

A Shared Services Foundation for Public Media

Beyond Individual Survival

With CPB funding eliminated and traditional revenue under pressure, public media needs collaborative infrastructure that individual stations cannot afford independently.

The Data Product as Shared Services Backbone

This AI platform becomes the foundation for services that transform operational costs across the network:

Revenue Diversification

Interactive website bots, transcription services, and audio processing become shared capabilities accessible to all member stations

Operational Efficiency

Shared systems and tools reduce individual station costs while improving capabilities

Network Infrastructure

AI-powered back-office services that save member stations thousands annually while contributing to the training corpus

Collective Resilience

Rather than competing for scarce resources, stations collaborate to build shared infrastructure that strengthens the entire public media ecosystem.

The Public Media Data Platform

Strategic Expansion

Extending this model to twenty or more major metropolitan markets across the United States

Platform Economics

  • Network Effects: Each additional market increases the platform's comprehensiveness and commercial value
  • Local Specialization: Regional stations contribute irreplaceable geographic and cultural expertise
  • Shared Infrastructure: Distributed costs make sophisticated AI capabilities accessible to individual stations

Revenue Model

  • Aggregate licensing agreements with major AI companies leveraging collective bargaining power
  • Managed service offerings to public media stations in major markets
  • Scalable value proposition that grows with platform adoption

Implementation Strategy
